An everyday mobile app for electricians

A mobile app which seamlessly gathers data and keeps it in one place, backed up to the cloud. The app is paired with a web front end where the user can download their data to their personal computers for more detailed analysis.

    Sizing the scope

    As a designer it is very important to understand the problem and the scope of the project. When the exploring area was defined we produced a solid hypothesis from the information available upon our previous experience and conversations with experts in the topic. From that premise we worked over zoom call and generated a set of interviews plan, from where we uncover key interest points to be analysed.

    Creating the hypotesis

    We assumed that if we help electricians perform on-site measurements and calculations on the job, we can help their work become more efficient. We also considered that helping electricians manage data points they gather (such as measurements, job requirements and tests) could save them time and work.

    Meeting the real people

    Online interviews were organized for a number of electricians to find out all daily pain points. From there, we found what data they capture day to day on jobsite, what tools they use to capture and store data and what pain points they have.

    Result

    Problem statement

    The user research indicated that many electricians rely on their memory, hand written notes and photos to capture data to plan, perform and evaluate their work. This is an unreliable process and it could be catastrophic if data is lost. Electricians need assistance in managing data for every stage of a project.

    Prototyping & Iteration

    User insights are classified and prioritized, possible competitors are evaluated then, a user flow is designed and the first prototype is created. A series of test are run at this stage, doing specific tasks for later iterations to the model.

    Gorilla test, 5 second test and A-B test techniques are use. Further analysis is done, to fulfil extra feedback surveys were ran through google surveys. Until the model fills users' needs and brings off satisfaction.

    Final version

    After last iteration the prototype is ready for hand out to developers. Some future considerations has been left for consecutive app versions where are constant open from improvements.
